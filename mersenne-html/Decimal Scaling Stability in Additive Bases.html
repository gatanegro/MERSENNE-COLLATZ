Decimal Scaling Stability in Additive Bases

Computational Discoveries and Conjectures
Based on collaborative computational exploration
Abstract

We report a computational discovery concerning integer representations as sums from fixed generating sets: certain additive bases exhibit decimal scaling stability, meaning that representations of an integer (N) can be systematically transformed into representations of (10^m N) for all (m \ge 0). Through extensive computational experiments, we document this phenomenon for Fibonacci numbers, Mersenne exponents, and a variety of heterogeneous dense sets. We identify persistent structural features of scaling chains, including term persistence, predictable migration of dominant summands, and sublinear growth in representation length. We prove a rigorous scaling theorem for Fibonacci numbers, formulate a general logarithmic coverage heuristic explaining the observed behavior, and state conjectures characterizing broad classes of scaling-stable additive bases. These results suggest a new dynamical perspective on additive representations and open several avenues for further theoretical and computational investigation.

Keywords: Additive bases; Fibonacci numbers; Zeckendorf representation; decimal scaling; computational number theory; greedy algorithms; conjectures
1. Introduction & Theoretical Framework
Theorem 3.2 (Fibonacci Decimal Scaling)

Let (F = {F_n}_{n\ge2}) denote the Fibonacci numbers. For every (N \in \mathbb{N}), there exists a constant (C_N) such that for all (m \ge 0), the integer (10^m N) can be expressed as a sum of Fibonacci numbers using at most (C_N + O(m)) summands.

Proof sketch: Let (N = \sum_i F_{a_i}) be its Zeckendorf representation. For each summand, replace (F_{a_i}) with (F_{a_i+5}), yielding a value approximately equal to (10N). The discrepancy between (10F_{a_i}) and (F_{a_i+5}) is bounded and can be corrected using a fixed finite set of small Fibonacci numbers. Iterating this procedure establishes the result.

Conjecture 4.1 (Mersenne Exponent Scaling)

The set of Mersenne exponents admits infinite decimal scaling chains for all positive integers, with representation length growing sublinearly in the number of decimal scalings.

Conjecture 5.3 (Scaling from Logarithmic Coverage)

Let (S \subset \mathbb{N}) satisfy logarithmic coverage and contain at least one bounded element. Then for every (N \in \mathbb{N}), the integers (10^m N) admit additive representations using elements of (S) for all (m \ge 0).
